AB  - Summary Home enteral tube feeding (HETF) should be used for residents who are unable to meet nutritional requirements through oral intake alone. It refers to the delivery of food directly into the gut via a tube. The types of enteral feeding most commonly used are nasogastric tubes (NG) and percutaneous endoscopic gastrostomy tubes (PEG). Tracheostomy is to protect or aid respiration. The natural ability to warm and humidify air during inspiration is bypassed with a tracheostomy in situ. Taking blood from a vein (venepuncture) is usually a straightforward procedure but can sometimes prove challenging in an elderly resident with fragile veins. The most common problem caused by osteoporosis is a fracture. The chapter also deals with fractures of the limbs. If a resident with a fracture or suspected fracture has persistent severe pain on using the limb in question, medical advice should be sought.
