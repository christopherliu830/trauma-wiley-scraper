AB  - Summary This chapter considers connections between the quirky sensibility of recent indie cinema and a broader realm of contemporary culture. It talks about the relationship between quirky culture and indie culture more generally. There are many ways of drawing connections between quirky filmmaking and other media. In film, the quirky is often recognizable via one or more of a number of conventions: a visual aesthetic that finds various ways of evoking a simplified and fastidious ?artificiality?, a comic register combining bathetic deadpan, comedy-of-embarrassment, and physical humor, and a thematic interest in childhood and innocence. As in film, tracing the quirky in contemporary music reveals the potentially crossgeneric nature of the sensibility, even as its iterations might all be categorized under the far broader ?sensibility? of indie within the medium. One of the most reliably quirky contemporary standup comedians is Kristen Schaal.
