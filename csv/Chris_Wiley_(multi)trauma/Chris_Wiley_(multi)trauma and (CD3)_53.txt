AB  - Summary The peritoneum is site to a wide spectrum of tumours and tumour-like conditions. The nomenclature of many of these conditions has evolved over recent years and there persists many morphological and immunophenotypical similarities between these tumours and non-neoplastic lesions. Primary tumours of the peritoneum are regarded as those with primary peritoneal manifestation in the absence of a visceral origin. They may be classified according to their probable histogenetic cell of origin: mesothelial, sub-mesothelial mesenchymal and uncertain (uncommitted stem cells). Accurate diagnosis is important for clinical prognostic and therapeutic reasons and, for malignant mesothelioma, for medicolegal reasons. The most common tumours to involve the peritoneal tissues are secondary tumours, especially carcinomatosis from primary gastrointestinal tract or ovarian neoplasms. Tumour-like lesions arising in the peritoneum encompass a variety of cysts of the omentum and mesentery, and pseudo-tumours as well as diverse metaplastic conditions. In this chapter there is a review of the various conditions, with emphasis on the diagnostic features and role of ancillary investigations that may be of value in discriminating between different lesions.
