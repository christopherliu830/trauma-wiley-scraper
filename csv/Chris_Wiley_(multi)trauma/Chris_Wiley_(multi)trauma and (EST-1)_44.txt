AB  - Summary Modernists reacted to the outbreak of the First World War in a variety of ways. Underlying differences of view about culture, society, and politics came to the fore both in immediate reactions to the conflict and in later accounts of it. In the broadest sense, a distinction can be made between writers and artists who became soldiers and those who remained civilians. One could refer to a number of writers in this context, but Katherine Mansfield was one who grasped the implications of the First World War more acutely than most. To most modernists who enlisted as soldiers, it was hard to see the pacifist position as anything more than a self-serving avoidance of duty. The impact of technology on the War is frequently registered in modernist writing about it. Anxieties about personal authenticity are inseparable from the question of gender in Mansfield's work.
