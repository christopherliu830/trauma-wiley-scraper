AB  - Summary The limitations of conventional methods for bone reconstruction led to the idea of processing widespread biogenic materials in order to obtain biomimetic bone substitutes. Results from studies of the past 50?years support the possibility to process shells and marble through different routes of synthesis. This chapter describes in detail the methods of obtaining hydroxyapatite powders from shells and marble. In the same time, a brief description of the possibilities for further processing of ceramics derived from marble and shells for the manufacture of implantable devices (pellets and scaffold sites), both by conventional and solid free-form (SFF) methods, is presented. Also, compositional, morphological, structural, and mechanical characterization of the materials are gradually presented for each processing step of the natural precursors. Finally, the in vitro and in vivo tests of the implantable products are presented. These evaluations have generated positive results but there are still a number of limitations related to variability of the precursor characteristics, the synthesis reactions progress, the sintering parameters, and the manufacturing routes of the devices. Also, the biological characterization results require confirmation in other studies. Beyond these limitations, the obtaining of bone substitutes derived from shells and marble will present favorable perspectives.
