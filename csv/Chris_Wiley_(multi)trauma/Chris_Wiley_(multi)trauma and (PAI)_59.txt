AB  - Summary In presenting the philosophy of the treatment staff to clients, clients can be encouraged to share more information in therapy. A variety of research studies also attest to the utility of relapse prevention and harm reduction strategies as applied to high-risk groups of offenders. This chapter describes in some detail an approach to assessment that has been found to be useful as well as the theoretical underpinnings to this model. An accurate estimate of risk is essential to a comprehensive assessment, as also the use of actuarially-based assessment instruments. The chapter describes specific measures to assess the specific treatment targets addressed in the sex offender treatment program, and to assess change related to those targets. These measures include: Bumby's Child Molester and Rape Cognitive Distortions Scales (Bumby scales), Buss?Durkee Hostility Inventory (BDHI), Coping in Stressful Situations Scale (CISS), and Coping Using Sex Inventory (CUSI).
