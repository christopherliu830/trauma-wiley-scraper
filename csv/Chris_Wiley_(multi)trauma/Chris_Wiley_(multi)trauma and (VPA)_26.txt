AB  - Summary This chapter summarizes the issues involved in determining the effects of medication on cognition and development in children. First, it discusses the mechanisms by which medication can affect cognition. Then, it considers what is known about the cognitive effects of the various classes of psychotropic medications used in pediatrics, considering adult studies if there are no data in children. Lastly, the chapter explores the complications involved in the assessment of change of cognition in children, and briefly describes the tools that have been used to assess change.
