AB  - Summary Portal hypertension drives the prognosis of cirrhosis leading to the development of the complications that define decompensation. Portal hypertension due to liver disease is classified as intrahepatic portal hypertension. This chapter presents a list of causes of portal hypertension based on this classification. Understanding of the pathophysiology of portal hypertension has greatly benefited from the development of animal models of cirrhosis and prehepatic portal hypertension. The knowledge gained has allowed for a successful transition into therapeutic strategies. The chapter focuses on the evaluation and diagnosis of portal hypertension in cirrhosis. Treatment of portal hypertension is different depending on the clinical scenario. The chapter then summarizes the natural history of chronic liver disease according to data obtained from a large prospective randomized controlled trial (RCT) and a systematic review.
