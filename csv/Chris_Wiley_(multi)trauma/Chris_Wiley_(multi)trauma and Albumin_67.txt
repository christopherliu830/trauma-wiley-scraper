AB  - SUMMARY This comprehensive revision article on convulsions in the newborn period is presented as an aid both to newcomers to the field of child neurology and to those readers who may find it helpful to have an up-to-date review of the subject. The author considers mainly two types of convulsions in the newborn period?clonic and tonic. The pathophysiology, aetiology, clinical pattern and management of convulsions are systematically discussed and, where possible, indications are given of the prognoses for infants who have convulsions from various causes during the first month of life. RÉSUMÉ L'article qui fait une révision générale sur les convulsions dans la période néonatale est écrit pour aider aussi bien les nouveaux venus dans le domaine de la neurologie infantile que les lecteurs qui souhaitent une mise au point à jour sur le sujet. L'auteur considère principalement deux types de convulsions dans la période néonatale?clonique et tonique. La physiopathologie, l'étiologie, les symptômes cliniques et le traitement des convulsions sont systématiquement discutés et lorsque cela est possible des indications sont données sur le pronostic des nourrissons qui souffrent de convulsions, de causes variées, durant le premier mois de la vie. ZUSAMMENFASSUNG Der vorliegende Übersichtsartikel über Neugeborenenkrämpfe soll sowohl den Neulingen auf dem Gebiet der Kinderneurologie als auch den Lesern, die sich an einem Überblick über die neusten Erkenntnisse auf diesem Gebiet orientieren wollen, als Hilfe an die Hand gegeben werden. Der Autor berücksichtigt hauptsächlich zwei Krampfformen in der Neugeborenen-periode?Klonisch und Tonisch. Pathophysiologie, Ätiologie, klinische Erscheinungsform und Behandlung der Krämpfe werden systematisch diskutiert und soweit möglich werden Hinweise auf die Prognosen für die Kinder gegeben, die während der ersten Lebensmonate an Krämpfen unterschiedlicher Ursache leiden. RESUMEN Este artículo en que se revisan las convulsiones del período neo-natal se presenta como una ayuda para los recién llegados a la neuropediatría y para los lectores que pueden encontrar útil una puesta al día del tema. El autor considera fundamentalmente dos tipos de convulsiones en el período neonatal: clónicas y tónicas. Se discute sistemáticamente la fisiopatología, etiologia, esquema clínico y tratamiento de las convulsiones y cuando es posible, se dan indicaciones sobre el pro-nóstico de los niños que padecen convulsiones por varias causas durante el primer mes de vida.

AB  - Reimbursement policies for health care services are greatly diminishing in the U.S. and Western Europe. Hence, there is an increasing need for doctors and other care givers to reduce costs without compromising the quality of the care being delivered. The clinical laboratory is viewed as an area of high costs where significant reductions have been targeted. Efficient utilization of laboratory services can be achieved by elimination of the general health panel, removal of old tests or those that provide redundant information, a reduction in the use of standing orders, more judicious use of drug assays, acceptance of clinical practice guidelines, and use of reflex testing algorithms. New technologies such as DNA probes can substantially improve diagnostic efficiency. Point-of-care testing devices which have higher costs than incremental central laboratory expenses should only be used if they reduce overall operating expenses. Implementation of expert systems can make remaining tests more effective. Doctors and laboratorians must collaborate to achieve more efficient utilization practices.

AB  - Summary This chapter focuses on the conditions more commonly encountered, either in medical examinations or in clinical practice, and attempts to provide a framework for developing an understanding of their pathological basis and diagnosis. Oral Glucose Tolerance Test (OGTT) can be used in the diagnosis of diabetes mellitus (DM) and to distinguish between the other causes of hyperglycaemia. Diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA) is a life-threatening acute complication of DM, occurring when insulin therapy is absent or becomes inadequate for the current physiological state. Diabetes insipidus (DI) is a condition associated with either abnormal antidiuretic hormone (ADH) secretion from the posterior pituitary (central) or an abnormal renal response to ADH. Cortisol is a corticosteroid hormone produced by the adrenal cortex. Cushing's syndrome is caused by an excess of glucocorticoids. Multiple endocrine neoplasia is a rare syndrome involving the development of either multiple endocrine gland tumours or hyperplasia.
