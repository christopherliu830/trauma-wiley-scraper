AB  - Summary Veterinary entomology is commonly used to describe the wider study of all arthropods parasitic on animals (ectoparasites), including arachnids such as ticks and mites. There are two major classes of arthropods of veterinary importance, namely the Insecta and Arachnida. The chapter summarizes the important orders in these classes, and tabulates families and genera of arthropods of veterinary importance. Arthropods are metameric, i.e. they are divided into segments. Segments have become fused into clusters, such as the head, thorax and to a lesser extent the abdomen. Each group of segments is specialised for functions different from those of the other parts of the body. Members of the class Insecta can be distinguished from the other arthropods by the presence of only three pairs of legs in the adults, and the broad division of the body into three sections: the head, thorax and abdomen.
