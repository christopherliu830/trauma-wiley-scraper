AB  - Summary The alimentary tract includes the oral cavity, esophagus, stomach, intestine, and rectum. These structures all share the same basic structure and consist of an epithelial-lined cavity supported by a submucosa. This submucosa is surrounded by bone in parts of the oral cavity and by muscle in the rest of tract. Because of this consistent basic structure, tumors that develop within the tubular alimentary tract can be subdivided into those of the epithelium and those that develop within the supporting mesenchymal tissue. Cancer staging requires assessment of regional lymph nodes for the presence of metastases. Papillomas of the oral cavity can be subdivided into squamous papillomas that are benign neoplasms and viral papillomas that are hyperplastic lesions caused by papillomavirus infection. Squamous cell carcinomas are the most common oral neoplasm of cats, horses, and the production animal species. They are the second most common malignant oral neoplasm of dogs.

AB  - Summary Polyps of the colon are defined as mucosal projections and encompass a wide range of lesions. The most common polyp is the conventional adenoma, which includes tubular, tubulovillous and villous adenoma. These are classified according to architectural features, and graded according to the severity of dysplasia. Some lesions show progression to adenocarcinoma, and these lesions must be carefully assessed histologically to determine whether conservative management is appropriate. More recently, it has been recognised that there are other pre-malignant polyps with a serrated cryptal architecture. These lesions encompass sessile serrated adenoma, mixed polyps and traditional serrated adenoma, and these may be related to at least some hyperplastic polyps. The progression of serrated adenomas to adenocarcinoma may involve different genetic pathways and these are under continued investigation. Polyposis syndromes associated with all of these lesions are described. Finally, other neoplastic and non-neoplastic polyps of variable frequency are outlined.
