AB  - Summary This chapter explores in detail the diseases which may require histologic assessment by the pathologist to confirm the diagnosis and establish the prognosis. It reviews briefly less-common conditions of the eyelid and conjunctiva and the nasolacrimal system that are referred to ophthalmologists for assessment, where histologic examination may or may not be completed. Dermoids are choriostomas, which are congenital, misplaced normal tissues that are most frequently located on the cornea and conjunctiva and occasionally on the eyelid skin. Hamartomas of the eyelid occur occasionally in dogs and humans, most commonly at the lateral canthus of the canine. Entropion and ectropion are common conformational abnormalities in dogs and are occasionally noted in cats and herbivores. Pyogranulomatous/granulomatous blepharitis is an idiopathic, nonseptic granulomatous nodular disease seen exclusively in dogs. Chalazion is a zonal granulomatous deep dermatitis that forms in response to exposure to tarsal gland secretions which are normally sequestered within the gland.

AB  - Abstract A layer of cells (the ?biomembrane?) has been identified in large segmental defects between bone and surgically placed methacrylate spacers or antibiotic-impregnated cement beads. We hypothesize that this contains a pluripotent stem cell population with potential valuable applications in orthopedic tissue engineering. Objectives using biomembranes harvested from rat segmental defects were to: (1) Culture biomembrane cells in specialized media to direct progenitor cells along bone or cartilage cell differentiation lineages; (2) evaluate harvested biomembranes for mesenchymal stem cell markers, and (3) define relevant gene expression patterns in harvested biomembranes using microarray analysis. Culture in osteogenic media produced mineralized nodules; culture in chondrogenic media produced masses containing chondroitin sulfate/sulfated proteoglycans. Molecular analysis of biomembrane cells versus control periosteum showed significant upregulation of key genes functioning in mesenchymal stem cell differentiation, development, maintenance, and proliferation. Results identified significant upregulation of WNT receptor signaling pathway genes and significant upregulation of BMP signaling pathway genes. Findings confirm that the biomembrane has a pluripotent stem cell population. The ability to heal large bone defects is clinically challenging, and novel tissue engineering uses of the biomembrane hold great promise in treating non-unions, open fractures with large bone loss and/or infections, and defects associated with tumor resection. ? 2012 Orthopaedic Research Society.
