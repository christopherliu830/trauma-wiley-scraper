AB  - Summary Since first gaining an EU license in 1996 to treat haemophiliacs with inhibitors, rFVIIa (NovoSeven?) has been used extensively for off-label indications. Between 2000 and 2008, off-label (including prophylactic) use of rFVIIa increased more than 140-fold and, in 2008, accounted for more than 97% of in-hospital use of the drug. Despite this, there is a paucity of evidence to support its off-label use. Several large randomized controlled trials (RCTs) have shown general trends towards a reduction in blood product administration but not an overall mortality benefit. Furthermore, when used off-license, an increase in thromboembolic events has been demonstrated. It is unclear if there is a role for rFVIIa in the critically ill, and it is likely that only a subset of patients will benefit. It is not a panacea for uncontrolled haemorrhage and, combined with the side effect profile and economic cost, careful consideration should be given to its off-label use.
