AB  - Summary Over one-quarter of all cancers in males are due to tumours of the kidney, bladder or prostate. Bladder cancer is now the second commonest urological malignancy with over 10,000 new cases annually in the UK and 45,000 new cases in the USA. Screening has increasingly been attempted; indeed, bladder cancer was probably the first solid tumour in males in which a systematic screening programme was seriously considered. Other rare sites of cancer include the urethra, penis and epididymis. There are substantial worldwide variations in the incidence of carcinoma of the prostate, which is among the commonest of all cancers in men. Prostatic carcinoma is often asymptomatic, and is increasingly diagnosed at routine rectal examination. Although surgery has traditionally been the cornerstone of treatment, both radiotherapy and cytotoxic chemotherapy are assuming increasing importance. Management approaches and guidelines have changed markedly during the past 25 years.
