AB  - Summary This chapter discusses the use of plasma for enhanced wound healing, treatment of gastrointestinal disorders, dental applications and treatment of cancer. Several studies have shown that thermal nitric-oxide-producing plasma is used for treatment of inflammatory and erosive processes in tissues within the pleural and abdominal cavities, lungs, stomach and bowels and ear-nose-throat (ENT) organs. The Dextran sodium sulfate (DSS) model was used to produce ulcerative colitis in mice, which is representative of human ulcerative colitis. A recent study of xenographted cancer cell also reported surprising efficacy and selectivity against several different types of cancer cells. In this study the researchers employed a Helium plasma jet. Effectiveness of the plasma nitric oxide (NO)-therapy has been demonstrated on chronic gingivitis using the Plazon plasma generator. Intense heat and even fire have been used in medicine to stop bleeding and prevent infection after injury or surgery.
