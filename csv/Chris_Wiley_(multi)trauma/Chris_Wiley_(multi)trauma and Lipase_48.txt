AB  - Summary Botulism in humans is caused predominantly by Clostridium botulinum groups I and II. Botulinum toxin has many medical applications in the treatment of neurological conditions and is also used very commonly for cosmetic purposes. It describes several types of botulism. The chapter discusses properties of C. botulinum and botulinum neurotoxins. The hazardous nature of proteolytic C. botulinum and non-proteolytic C. botulinum, and the extreme potency of the botulinum neurotoxins require that all practical work is restricted to containment laboratories that afford a high degree of worker protection. The ability of spores of C. botulinum to survive heat treatment and the effect of temperature, pH, salt concentration and other factors on growth of the bacteria determine the methods used to prevent survival and growth of C. botulinum in foods.
