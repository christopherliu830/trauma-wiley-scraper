AB  - Summary In humans, the process of olfaction begins when volatile molecules enter the nasal cavity and activate receptors in the olfactory epithelium (OE). The olfactory receptor (OR) proteins are located in hair-like projections of the olfactory sensory neurons (OSNs). Activation of the olfactory receptor (OR) proteins sets in chain a complex sequence of biochemical reactions. The signals from the OR cells responding to odorants and their metabolites are picked up by the olfactory bulb (OB). The signal patterns leaving the OB then travel to the piriform cortex (PC). Output from the PC goes to various other brain regions and, combined with other inputs, is eventually interpreted as an odour percept in the orbitofrontal cortex (OFC). This chapter describes the human olfaction process in the simplified above-described sequence, and compares the process with that found in other species. It also discusses second messenger cascade and anosmia.
