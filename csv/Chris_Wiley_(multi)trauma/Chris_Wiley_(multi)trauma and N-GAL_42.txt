AB  - Summary The use of osseointegrated dental implants in the treatment of fully and partially edentulous patients has proven to be a highly successful treatment modality. However, complications with implants and/or supported prostheses have occurred, and their prevalence has substantially grown with the increased use of dental implants in clinical practice over the last several years. As is the case in many areas of medicine and dentistry, avoiding a problem through appropriate prevention applies also to dental implant treatment. The interpretation of the causes of complications has enhanced the understanding of the etiology of biological and mechanical complications with implants and implant-assisted prostheses and has helped to develop recommendations on how to reduce the risk of their occurrence. Comprehensive diagnosis, treatment planning and risk assessment are critical components to avoid or at least reduce that risk. It is the purpose of this chapter to outline these critical steps in substantial detail.
