AB  - Summary Development of an anesthetic protocol for any ophthalmic patient involves not only appropriate anesthetic drug selection, but also a management plan to ensure an optimal postoperative outcome. This requires knowledge of the patient's physical status and the specific ophthalmic procedure to be performed. It also requires familiarity with ophthalmic physiology and the effects of current ophthalmic medications. Selection of an anesthetic protocol for ophthalmic surgery should include consideration of the effects on intraocular pressure (IOP), pupil size, globe position, tear production, and the potential for initiation of the oculocardiac reflex (OCR) during surgical manipulation of the globe. Pupil size as an indicator of anesthetic depth is not reliable. Topical ophthalmic preparations are concentrated medications that may cause systemic side-effects, especially when administered to very small patients. The electroretinogram (ERG) is a widely used electrodiagnostic test in veterinary ophthalmology, with the flash ERG (FERG) being most useful in detecting diffuse retinal disorders.
