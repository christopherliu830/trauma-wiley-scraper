AB  - Summary This book is focused on the assessment of a stroke patient linking the neurological findings with the evaluation of vessels, and on integration of these findings with parenchymal and perfusion imaging. The neurological symptom onset was long equated with the timing of a thromboembolic occlusion. In reality, it is not. Symptom onset is the time of the collateral or residual flow depletion to a level causing the neuronal dysfunction. Structured case-based problem solving is provided to illustrate how to approach typical patient problems, what key questions we ask to begin patient management, and the complimentary use of diverse ultrasound tests to the neurological examination and the standard or care CT or MRI scanning across different pathogenic mechanisms. Early determination of the stroke mechanism is the key to treatment selection.
