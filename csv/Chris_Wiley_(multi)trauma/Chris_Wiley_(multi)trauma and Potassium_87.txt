AB  - Summary This chapter helps the reader recognise that: nutrition is a demand-led process; nutrition is integrative and complex; and the complexity is due to the interaction between the metabolic system and the external system. It outlines some fundamental principles of nutrition, explaining how they contribute to building and maintaining the complex systems that represent the function of the body, and how the systems through which food is extracted from the environment can and do exert influence on this. These principles act as a foundation on which it is possible to consider different aspects of nutrition research methodology. The authors also highlight specific limitations of their conceptual understanding that need to be addressed. The underlying objective of nutrition research is to understand the needs of individuals, groups and populations for energy and nutrients, and how these might best be met from dietary intake and through other processes.

AB  - Summary A variety of diseases can affect the kidneys leading to a sudden deterioration in renal function (acute kidney injury) or an irreversible deterioration of renal function (chronic kidney disease). The selection of renal replacement therapy (RRT) in acutely ill children depends upon the availability of treatment modalities and ventilatory support, the patient's requirements for fluid and solute removal and haemodynamic stability. Cardiovascular abnormalities develop early in the course of CKD and progress as end stage is reached. Protein energy wasting (PEW) is characterised by the state of decreased body protein mass and fuel reserves. Carnitine is an amino acid derivative which has a key role in the regulation of fatty acid metabolism and adenosine triphosphate (ATP) formation. Congenital nephrotic syndrome (CNS) is a rare inherited kidney disorder. Nephrogenic diabetes insipidus (NDI) is a rare genetic disorder characterised by the kidney failing to respond to arginine vasopressin, the antidiuretic hormone (ADH).

Improvements in immunosuppressive therapy, surgical techniques, and organ preservation have permitted utilization of transplantation in a greater number of patients, and pediatric patients in particular have benefited from the growth of transplantation.
