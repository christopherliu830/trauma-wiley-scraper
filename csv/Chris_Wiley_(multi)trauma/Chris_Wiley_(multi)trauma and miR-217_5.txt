AB  - Summary Most studies of subversive aesthetics take political modernism as their point of departure. Modernism was too often premised on a stagist mythos whereby the new simply replaces the old, whereas in fact the old and the archaic can be mobilized in favor of the new and the radical. Rather than search for ?new stories,? ?new techniques,? and ?new apps,? artists/theorists can make old stories new by reimagining them through alternative artistic traditions with ancient roots. This vast planetary archive of ideas and strategies forms a kind of aesthetic commons. This chapter discusses the artistic strategies that engage the aesthetic commons, the long-standing archive of genres, techniques, and texts perpetually available for recombinant creativity. It presents illustrations of films made on the aesthetic common, such as Godard's monumental metafilm Histoire(s) du Cinema, Perkin's One Night the Moon, and Franny Armstrong's drama?documentary?animation?essay film The Age of Stupid.
